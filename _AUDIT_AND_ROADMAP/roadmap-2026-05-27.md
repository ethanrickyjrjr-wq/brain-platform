## LONG-TERM — 3–12 months

Each move turns correlation into causation, or manual runs into an autonomous system.

1. **Outcomes loop wired up.** Predictions table seeded in §6.1.4. Now build the cron that grades predictions against observed values; surface drift; flag brains whose direction has been systematically wrong. This is what makes "we have 50 brains" different from "we have 50 dashboards." The seed corpus for everything downstream — backtests, fine-tuning, drift detection.
2. **Causal layer (§8.1).** Instrumental variable analysis using Hurricane Ian as the exogenous shock to corridor supply. Synthetic control corridors. Difference-in-differences for policy effects. Lives in `refinery/causal/`. Consumed by domain brains, never directly by master.
3. **Backtests (§8.2).** Every derived metric (Corridor Factor, Seasonal Credit Risk, future metrics) tested against 2022–2024 outcomes. Drop anything that doesn't predict above baseline.
4. **Scheduled runs + watch-list + real-time subscriptions (§8.3–§8.5).** Cron-driven 3am refinery: FRED pulse, permit deltas, TDT release window, SBA quarterly, NOAA hurricane track. Brief waits in inbox by 7am. Where push exists (webhook, SSE), use it; where it doesn't, tight-poll. Downstream brains rebuild automatically when source data changes.
5. **Regional expansion — the post-character-generator vision.** FL-other-cities (Tampa, Orlando, Jacksonville) fact-pack inputs feeding comparison context into SWFL characters. Then FL statewide anchor (`macro-florida` reuse). Then national anchor (FRED/BLS national/Census ACS national). Then outlier brain — once national + state + regional fact packs sit in the same shape, divergences become SQL-computable. Each step has a gate; nothing starts until the prior cycle proves out.
6. **Multi-tenant /vault (BYO overlay).** Companies overlay their own asset data (rent rolls, P&L, leasing, capex, debt) on top of SWFL fact packs. Blocked by the same multi-tenant prereqs that block /vault from MCP v1: `user_id` column + RLS migration on `personal_vault.vault_fragments` + billing + schema isolation. Not a 2026 conversation, but it's the wedge.
7. **Multi-agent inference (§8.6).** Each brain runs as its own Claude agent in parallel; master agent consumes their OUTPUT blocks. Same DAG, at inference time instead of batch. Faster, fresher, more responsive to ad-hoc questions.
8. **Fine-tuned synthesis model (§8.7).** Outcomes table from §6.1.4 becomes training data. Constitution stops being a prompt and starts being weights. Anti-inference-bait rules stop being regex and start being learned behavior.

## NORTH STAR (the bet)

A homebuyer, a CRE analyst, a city planner, a journalist, a small operator, a parent picking a school — they can hold three variables in their head when they make a real decision about a real place. We can hold fifty, weighted honestly, with a quoted citation chain. **Math is easy. Weighting is everything.** A war starts in Iran, crude is going to $7, retail gas is about to double — the math hasn't changed but the decision has. That's the gas tank. Brains is the apparatus that recognizes the shockwave coming and weights every other brain against it.

Today we have 15 brains and master is an index. By the end of NEXT we'll have 15 brains, master is a synthesizer, and tourism-tdt is fully self-owned. By the end of NEAR-TERM we'll have a constitution, Yager-DST, 4–7 audience voices, and a report-page side channel. By the end of LONG-TERM we'll have an outcomes loop telling us when we're wrong, a causal layer telling us why, scheduled runs that don't need a human to fire them, and a regional expansion pattern that turns one SWFL into Tampa + Orlando + statewide.

That's the road.
